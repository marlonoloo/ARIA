# WHO-Aligned Emergency Triage Protocol (Demo Excerpt)

> DEMO DATA. Paraphrased, illustrative protocol content for the Project ARIA
> proof-of-concept. Not a substitute for official WHO/IMAI guidance.

## Triage severity bands

- **Critical** — immediate life threat. Examples: not breathing or obstructed
  airway, active heavy bleeding, unresponsive, seizure in progress, signs of
  shock (cold extremities, weak fast pulse, capillary refill > 3s).
  Action: resuscitate immediately, summon senior clinician, do not queue.
- **High** — emergency signs requiring care within minutes. Examples: severe
  respiratory distress, chest pain with sweating, severe dehydration, pregnancy
  with bleeding or severe abdominal pain. Action: move to front of queue.
- **Moderate** — priority signs, assess within the hour. Examples: high fever in
  a young child, persistent vomiting, moderate dehydration.
- **Low** — routine, can wait safely. Examples: minor wounds, mild cough,
  stable chronic-condition reviews.

## Pregnancy-related emergency signs

Vaginal bleeding, severe abdominal pain, severe headache with visual changes,
convulsions, or reduced fetal movement in later pregnancy are HIGH or CRITICAL.
Escalate to a clinician immediately and prepare for possible referral.

## Burns — rapid assessment

- Assess depth (superficial, partial, full thickness) and % body surface area.
- Burns to face, hands, feet, genitals, or circumferential burns are HIGH
  priority regardless of size.
- Cool with clean running water, do not apply ice; cover with clean dressing.

## Dehydration signs

Sunken eyes, lethargy, skin pinch returning slowly, and reduced urine output
indicate significant dehydration. In children this escalates severity quickly.
