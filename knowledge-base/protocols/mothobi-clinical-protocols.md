# Mothobi Healthcare Group — In-Clinic Protocols (Demo Excerpt)

> DEMO DATA. Fictional internal protocols for the Project ARIA proof-of-concept.

## Scope of practice

Most Mothobi clinics are staffed by nurses and clinical officers, not doctors.
Recommendations must stay within that scope and flag anything that requires
physician review or referral to a district hospital.

## Cultural / metaphorical symptom interpretation

Patients frequently describe symptoms metaphorically or in a non-native
language. Interpret common phrasings, but always confirm with the clinician:

- "My stomach is burning" — may mean epigastric pain (gastritis/ulcer/reflux),
  generalised abdominal pain, or anxiety. Clarify location, timing vs meals.
- "My heart is running" / "heart is jumping" — often palpitations or anxiety;
  rule out fever, dehydration, anaemia.
- "Hot body" — usually subjective fever; confirm with temperature.
- "Pressing headache" / "head is tight" — tension-type vs hypertension; check
  blood pressure, especially in pregnancy.

## Referral triggers (refer to district hospital)

- Any CRITICAL triage band.
- Suspected obstetric emergency.
- Burns meeting HIGH criteria (see WHO triage excerpt).
- Conditions outside nurse/clinical-officer scope or needing imaging/surgery.

## Patient communication

- Communicate results and next steps in the patient's preferred language.
- For low-literacy patients, prefer spoken (voice) instructions over written.
- Confirm understanding by asking the patient to repeat the next step.

## Documentation

Record chief complaint, assessment, actions taken, and the clinician decision
(accepted / modified / rejected) for every AI-supported consultation. This
audit trail is mandatory.
